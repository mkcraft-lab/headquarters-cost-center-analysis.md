# Why Organizations Should Not Depend on Manager Luck

## Background

In this organization, the quality of each site depended heavily on the local manager.

Some managers:

- Solved problems quickly
- Secured budget and support
- Communicated effectively with headquarters
- Protected frontline staff

Other managers:

- Delayed decisions
- Blocked communication
- Ignored operational problems
- Focused only on maintaining the existing system

As a result, employees often described the organization as “manager luck.”

Two sites could have the same resources, the same number of staff, and the same formal rules, yet perform completely differently depending on who was in charge.

This case study examines why organizations become dependent on personalities instead of systems, and why that creates instability.
