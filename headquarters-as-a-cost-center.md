# Headquarters as a Cost Center: When Support Functions Stop Supporting

## Background

In many organizations, headquarters exists to support frontline operations.

Typical headquarters functions include:

- Finance
- HR
- Procurement
- Strategy
- Training
- Operational support

However, in this organization, headquarters gradually became larger and more expensive, while providing less support to the sites that generated revenue.

The organization had approximately 800 employees, yet headquarters alone had roughly 30 staff members and annual headquarters-related costs of approximately 200 million yen.

These costs included:

- Administrative labor costs
- Overtime
- Travel and entertainment expenses
- Overseas recruitment-related programs
- Housing and living support for recruits
- Large-scale overseas visits and training programs

At the same time:

- Repair budgets at local sites were delayed
- Important equipment could not be replaced
- Frontline employees lacked support
- Financial pressure increased across the organization

This case study examines how a headquarters function can become a cost center instead of a support function.
